Self-Referential Chains and the Signal Anchoring Constraint

Research Paper

Version 13 — 2026

This paper proposes that knowledge systems drift when interpretations increasingly reference prior interpretations rather than reconnecting to the original signal they attempt to preserve. The Signal Anchoring Constraint — F ≈ A / (L × C) — formalizes this structural dynamic and connects it to cybernetics, predictive processing neuroscience, institutional failure, and AI alignment.

Why this paper matters: This paper introduces a structural explanation for why knowledge systems drift over time. By connecting cybernetics, predictive processing neuroscience, institutional failures, and AI model collapse, it shows that many modern alignment problems share the same underlying mechanism: interpretive systems losing contact with the signals they were designed to preserve.
