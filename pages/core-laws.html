      Core Laws

      These are the near-mathematical or law-like statements of Alignment Theory. They describe recurring structural dynamics across persons, systems, religion, and culture.

      Return to Framework Go to Essays

      Law 1 — Low agency increases steerability.

      Law 2 — Truth cannot be integrated faster than regulatory capacity allows.

      Law 3 — External control scales faster than internal regulation, but degrades coherence over time.

      Law 4 — Internal regulation scales slower than external control, but preserves coherence over time.

      Law 5 — Meaning cannot survive forced integration.

      Law 6 — Certainty adopted before integration functions as control rather than truth.

      Law 7 — Identity hardening is a compensatory response to sustained overload.

      Law 8 — Systems that punish uncertainty produce performative certainty.

      Law 9 — The more a system relies on surveillance, the less it can rely on conscience.

      Law 10 — When internal states are externalized into metrics, behavior adapts to the metric and the metric begins distorting the state it claims to measure.

      Law 11 — False systems replace inward reality with externally legible substitutes.

      Law 12 — Some realities can only be approached indirectly; when made into direct performance targets, they collapse into simulation.

      This applies especially to realities such as alignment, love, humility, sincerity, trust, and meaning.
